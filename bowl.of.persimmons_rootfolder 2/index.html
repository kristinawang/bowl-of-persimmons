Bowl of Persimmons

“I think you travel to search and you come back home to find yourself there.”
— Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

“Bowl of Persimmons” is a mini-illustration series influenced by the people, places, and the minutiae of everyday life that have shaped my notion of home.

Home is having a daily routine.

Home is visiting the farmers market with grandma every week.

Home is snuggles with my cat.

Home is where my own bed is.

Home is being around people who make you laugh one moment but drive you crazy the next...

Home is the smell of freshly baked banana bread.

Home is picking persimmons from mom's persimmon tree.

Home is a place that evokes a sigh of relief as I walk in the door.

Home is where I binge watch Downton Abbey without anyone judging me.

Home is starting my own urban garden in my tiny city apartment.

Home is a magical place called "the fridge".

Home is a place where I can see myself living in one day.

Kristina Shutian Wang is a daydreamer, chronic doodler, freelance designer, and full-time coffee fiend.
